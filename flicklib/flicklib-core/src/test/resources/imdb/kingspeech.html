Storyline

Tells the story of the man who became King George VI, the father of Queen Elizabeth II. After his brother abdicates, George ('Bertie') reluctantly assumes the throne. Plagued by a dreaded stammer and considered unfit to be king, Bertie engages the help of an unorthodox speech therapist named Lionel Logue. Through a set of unexpected techniques, and as a result of an unlikely friendship, Bertie is able to find his voice and boldly lead the country through war. Written by Anonymous

Did You Know?

Trivia

The film features a number of characters who have all been monarchs of Great Britain and these total to four. All of these are from the House of Windsor, and all four are the last four reigning monarchs of the Commonwealth. They are King George V (1910-1936) played by Michael Gambon, King Edward VIII (1936-1936) played by Guy Pearce, King George VI (1936-1952) played by Colin Firth and Queen Elizabeth II (1952-current) as a child, Princess Elizabeth, played by Freya Wilson. The latter is the only one of these monarchs who is not seen during their reigning period in the film. See more »

Goofs

Factual errors: Queen Elizabeth II has blue eyes in real life. Freya Wilson, who plays Princess Elizabeth, has brown eyes. See more »
